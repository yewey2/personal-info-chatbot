# 🌟 Mini-Project Assignment

**Manulife Asia AI**

## 🚀 Welcome to Project AMA (Ask Me Anything)

Interviews can feel repetitive—you introduce yourself again and again, hoping each time goes just right. What if you could flip the script?

**Project AMA** is your chance to build an AI-powered “interview-you” assistant. Think of it as a 24/7 digital version of you that can answer questions from recruiters, hiring managers, or anyone who wants to know your story. This mini-project gives you room to be creative, technical, and a little playful.

Let’s break down what you’ll be working on.

---

### 🧠 1. Knowledge Base (20%)

Your AMA bot needs to know *you*. Build a compact but rich knowledge base that forms the “brain” of your solution.

Consider including:

- Your résumé
- Your GitHub projects
- Your LinkedIn profile
- Blog posts or public write-ups
- Any other content that represents your work or personality

You don’t have to integrate every source perfectly. Focus on showing your thinking, your approach, and a practical plan for expansion. At minimum, your résumé must be included.

---

### 🛠️ 2. Back-End Design (50%)

This is where your technical creativity shines.

Answer questions like:

- Should this be a **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** system?
- Should it behave more like an **agentic workflow**?
- Will you tap into **commercial models** (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) or **open-source models** (Hugging Face, etc.)?
- What compute environment will you use—local, Colab, Kaggle?

We’re far more interested in your reasoning, architecture, coding style, and fresh ideas than fully optimized output. Use comments and notes to explain decisions and highlight possible improvements.

---

### 🧪 3. Testing & Evaluation (30%)

An AMA bot that makes things up about you? Hard pass.

Show us how you would ensure:

- The bot stays **accurate, reliable, and aligned**
- Your evaluation dataset is created (or generated!)
- Your testing scenarios reflect real interview-style prompts
- You measure the right **data science metrics** (e.g., precision/recall, factual consistency, robustness)
- You consider latencies and user experience

Testing is **critical**, especially in regulated industries like insurance—so give this section the attention it deserves.

---

### 🎨 4. Front-End (Bonus)

Totally optional, but a great way to stand out.

You can:

- Keep it simple with a command-line interface
- Build a small web UI
- Add UX touches that make the bot delightful to talk to

This is your playground—have fun if you decide to explore it!
